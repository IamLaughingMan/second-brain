## Best Fill Types for Side Sleepers

**Shredded memory foam:** Our top recommendation. Adjustable loft, pressure-relieving, conforms to head and neck. Most adjustable pillows let you add or remove fill to reach your ideal height.

**Solid latex:** Springier than memory foam, stays cool, very durable. Provides consistent loft without compressing. Slightly less contouring than foam.

**Down or down alternative:** Works for side sleepers with narrow to medium shoulders. May compress over time for heavier heads or broader frames. Choose high-loft down-filled options.

**Buckwheat:** Excellent support and cooling. Moldable to head shape. Heavy and makes noise when moving, not for light sleepers.

## Between-the-Knees: The Other Side Sleeper Pillow

A slim knee pillow placed between the knees while side sleeping reduces hip rotation and takes pressure off the lower spine. This is separate from your head pillow but equally important for hip and lower back health in side sleepers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What loft height should side sleepers look for in a pillow?

Side sleepers need a pillow high enough to fill the space between the shoulder and head, keeping the spine neutral. For most adults, a loft of 4–6 inches works. Broader shoulders need more loft; narrower frames need less. Adjustable pillows let you customize.

### Should side sleepers use a firm or soft pillow?

Medium to medium-firm is best for most side sleepers. Soft pillows compress too much, dropping the head and straining the neck. Very firm pillows can push the head up unnaturally. Medium-firm provides enough resistance to maintain alignment without discomfort.

### What is the best pillow material for side sleepers?

Memory foam (especially shredded) and latex are the most popular for side sleepers. Both provide consistent support without flattening overnight. Shredded memory foam is adjustable; solid latex is more durable. Down and down alternative can work for lighter sleepers if loft is sufficient.

### Can a pillow cause shoulder pain in side sleepers?

Yes. A pillow too low causes the shoulder to compress and the neck to curve downward. A pillow too high pushes the head up, straining the upper cervical spine. Proper pillow loft for side sleepers directly affects shoulder and neck health.

### How often should side sleepers replace their pillows?

Every 1–2 years for most pillow types. Memory foam pillows last 2–3 years. Down pillows can last up to 5 years with proper care. Test: if the pillow does not spring back when folded, it has lost adequate loft.
