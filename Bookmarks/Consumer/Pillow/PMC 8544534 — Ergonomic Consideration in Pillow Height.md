## Full Content (archived)

## Abstract
"Sleep problems have become one of the current serious public health issues. Pillow height affects the alignment of the cervical spine" and is closely connected to mechanical spine support. The authors examined research since 1997 on pillow height determinants, presenting quantitative measures including cervical spine alignment, body dimensions, contact pressure, and muscle activity. However, "the suggested range for achieving optimal cervical spine alignment, appropriate pressure distribution, and minimal muscle activity during sleep cannot yet be identified."

## Pillow Height Parameters Discussed

**Body Dimensions Measured:**
- Width from ear to shoulder (lateral position support height)
- Length from hindbrain to wall (supine position support height)
- Length from neck to wall (supine neck support)
- Head girth and shoulder length

**Note:** The document does NOT discuss mattress firmness or body weight as primary pillow height determinants. These were excluded from the pillow-specific analysis.

**Recommended Heights Found (cited from other studies):**
- 10 cm for supine position (Kim et al.)
- 7 cm for supine position (Li et al.)
- 10 cm for lateral position (Sacco et al.)

## Methodology

The authors conducted a systematic literature review of articles published 1997-2021, searching PubMed, Web of Science, ScienceDirect, and Scopus. They extracted data on subject demographics, pillow characteristics, measurement methods, and outcomes. Studies involved healthy adults (primarily 20-30 years old) in controlled laboratory settings, measuring parameters through radiography, pressure-sensing mats, and electromyography.

## Conclusion

"A comprehensive evaluation combining the above determinants provides a unique solution for ergonomic pillow design and proper pillow height selection." The authors stress that establishing optimal parameter ranges remains essential, and "a reasonable algorithm must be developed to weigh multiple determinants" for standardized pillow evaluation.
