## Key Takeaways
- **核心 trick**：剪窄 pillow width 由 20" → 16" (length 維持 22")，keeps shredded 填料唔 shift 去頂邊
- **材料**：AB Lifestyles 嘅 shredded Serene foam pillow（買 2 個拆 1 個攞夠填料）
- **靈感**：buckwheat pillow 傳統就係細寬度，原因一樣（防 fill migration）
- **做法**：拆現有 pillow → 倒空填料 → 剪原 inner shell 至 22"x16" → 車口 → 倒返填料調 firmness → 車埋 → 套 cover
- **強烈建議加 zipper** 喺 inner shell，方便日後 fine-tune fill（作者承認懶冇加）
- **填料量**：作者用 1.5 個原裝 pillow 嘅填料整 1 個新枕；後來整咗第二個放露營車

## Full Content (archived)

**TLDR**: I know how to sew, and I made a pillow from an existing shredded Serene foam pillow. I kept the standard length of about 22 inches but reduced the width to about 16 inches, so the pillow was narrower. Reducing the width kept the filling from shifting to the top of the pillow throughout the night.

I have a very popular post where I have reviewed lots of pillows as a side sleeper who sleeps hot. The pillow I've liked the most still available is the **Pluto Pillow**, as it was customized for me. However, it's just a little firmer than I would like. Several months ago, I bought some pillows from **AB Lifestyles filled with shredded Serene foam**. I've generally liked Serene foam pillows because they're cooler and more supportive than standard doughy memory foam. The Pluto has a Serene foam core.

These pillows were initially too soft, so I bought an extra one to get more foam. Making it firmer helped, but I kept noticing that the foam would shift to the top of the pillow overnight, making the pillow too flat for side sleeping. I noticed that **buckwheat pillows come in smaller sizes with shorter widths to prevent this**, so I figured it would work for foam, too.

I emptied the contents of the existing pillows, cut and sewed the existing inner shell to measure **22x16**, and then stuffed it until it reached the firmness and loft I wanted. Once I got that, I sewed it up and altered a pillow protector and cover to fit it perfectly.

So far, it's been exactly what I've been looking for. I get support all night because the foam doesn't shift much. I liked it so much, I made a second one that I keep in my camper. I think I just have a very specific way I want pillows to feel, and that's why customization has been the best solution for me.
