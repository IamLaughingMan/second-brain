# Company Brand Strategy

**HYGIEIAS — Company Master-Brand Strategy (v1.0)**
*Scope: the company brand across both lines (Foundations + Longevity). Not a product-specific document.*

---

## 1. Brand Idea & Promise

**Brand idea:** *Supplements, decoded.*

HYGIEIAS exists to end the gap between what a supplement actually is and what its label tells you. We sell the molecule, not the marketing. Every product is published as a full spec sheet — molecule, form, dose, purity %, lot-matched proof, and price per dose — so the customer never has to take our word for anything.

**Master line (the rallying cry):** *The molecule, not the marketing.*

**Company descriptor (locked, sits under the wordmark):** *Supplements, decoded.*

**The promise (one sentence, claims-safe):**
> Every HYGIEIAS product shows its molecule, its form, its dose, its purity %, its lot-matched Certificate of Analysis, and its price per dose — from daily foundations to the NAD+ frontier.

**What the promise commits us to (and what it deliberately does not):**
- It commits us to **facts and limits**: stating exactly what is in the bottle and exactly how strong the evidence is.
- It does **not** promise outcomes. We make no disease, anti-aging, or cure claims. This is a structure/function brand operating under DSHEA (Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act) and FTC (Federal Trade Commission) standards. Stating verifiable facts and honest limits is inherently the safest possible posture — the promise *is* the compliance strategy.

**Why this promise is defensible:** transparency is not a feature we bolt onto the page — it is the company's operating system. The promise is auditable (we publish a versioned Transparency Standard and grade our own evidence), house-wide (the same proof system on every SKU), and concentrated in one name. That makes it hard to copy and impossible to fake.

## 2. Company-Level Positioning — The Transparency Spine

**Positioning statement:**
> For smart, skeptical health buyers who are tired of hype and hidden labels, HYGIEIAS is the supplement house that publishes the full truth about every product — the exact molecule and form, the assayed purity, the lot-matched lab report, the honest evidence grade, and the real price per dose — across both everyday foundations and the longevity frontier. Unlike practitioner-premium brands that gate or omit their lab reports and lifestyle brands that show sourcing but not purity, HYGIEIAS puts the Certificate of Analysis and the price math on *every* product page, in *both* tiers, as the brand itself.

**The model (locked):** the The Ordinary / Deciem decoded-commodity register — calm, literal, lab-sheet plain, numerate, anti-hype, treating the customer as intelligent. We translate The Ordinary's "Active + Concentration %" naming into the supplement-native **"Ingredient + Form/Salt + Dose,"** because in supplements the *form* is where cheap brands hide quality.

**The white space we own (uncontested):** no broad branded house currently puts a lot-matched COA (Certificate of Analysis) + stated purity % + price-per-dose on *every* product detail page across *both* a foundational tier and a longevity tier.
- Practitioner-clinical brands (Thorne, Pure Encapsulations, Life Extension) have real rigor but **gate, omit, or only-on-request** their COAs.
- DTC-lifestyle brands (Ritual; the now-dead Care/of) lead on design and sourcing-transparency but are **foundational-only** with no purity %/COA-on-page.
- The value leader (NOW Foods) has elite labs but **downmarket branding and no public batch COAs**.
- The NMN/NAD+ natives (NOVOS, DoNotAge, Renue, Tru Niagen) do purity % + COA well but are **single-vertical** with no foundational range.

We sit deliberately **between NOW (cheap but opaque/downmarket) and Thorne/Pure/Momentous (premium but COA-gated):** *fair price, shown math.*

> *(Note: the competitor COA/transparency characterizations above are internal assessments based on public pages as of mid-2026, not sourced facts in our research file. Re-verify before any **comparative customer-facing** use, and keep on-store comparisons to category labels — "premium" / "budget" — rather than named brands, to limit comparative-advertising risk.)*

### The Five Decodes — the repeatable proof system on EVERY product, both lines

This is the spine made concrete. The same identical monospace block appears on every product detail page and every label.

1. **THE MOLECULE** — ingredient + form/salt + exact dose per serving (e.g. *"Magnesium Glycinate · 200 mg elemental"*). Naming the form is the flex.
2. **THE PURITY** — assayed purity % as a number (never "high quality"). Category-specific where relevant (e.g. omega-3 adds TOTOX / Total Oxidation value + exact EPA/DHA mg).
3. **THE PROOF** — lot-matched COA pulled by batch via **/verify**, plus a named third-party certification *where held* (USP Verified / NSF / Clean Label Project). Never the empty phrase "third-party tested."
4. **THE PRICE** — price-per-dose *and* price-per-gram, computed and shown, with a one-line *"priced on formulation cost, not marketing"* note.
5. **THE REASONING** — a one-line **form rationale** (*"glycinate for absorption + GI tolerance, not cheap oxide"*) and an honest **Evidence Grade** (Strong / Moderate / Early).

## 3. Brand Story — Customer-Facing Draft (English, claims-safe)

> *The following is a public-facing draft for the About page / Amazon A+ / PDP intro. It is written structure/function only, with no disease, anti-aging, or cure language. It must clear the claims-review gate before publication.*

**We think you can handle the truth about what's in the bottle.**

Most supplement labels are built to be admired, not understood. They lead with promises and bury the facts — the exact form of the ingredient, how pure it really is, how much you're actually getting per dose, and what it costs per gram. The important numbers are the ones that go missing.

HYGIEIAS was built the other way around.

We're named after Hygieia, the Greek goddess of health — the figure behind the bowl-and-serpent symbol you still see on pharmacies. That's the only romance you'll find here. Everything else is plain: we name our products after the molecule, the form, and the dose, because the name should *be* the spec sheet.

On every product, we publish the same five things — what we call the Decodes: **the molecule** (ingredient, form, and exact dose), **the purity** (a real assayed number, not an adjective), **the proof** (the lot-matched lab report for the exact batch you bought, looked up at our **/verify** page), **the price** (per dose and per gram, with the math shown), and **the reasoning** (why we chose that form, and an honest grade of how strong the science actually is).

That last one matters most. We grade our own evidence — Strong, Moderate, or Early — and we don't hide the ones marked Early. On our longevity products especially, we'll tell you plainly: **the research is real but still early — and we'll show you exactly how early.** We'd rather lose a sale than oversell a molecule.

We make two kinds of products under one name. **Foundations** are the daily essentials — creatine, magnesium, vitamin D3+K2, omega-3 — the well-studied basics, priced fairly with the math in the open. **Longevity** is the healthspan frontier, including the NAD+ system, written in our most careful voice. Same lab report. Same standard. Same honesty.

Everything we do is governed by one public, versioned promise — **the HYGIEIAS Transparency Standard** — that lists the rules every product must meet before it can ship. You can read it. You can hold us to it. That's the point.

*These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. These products are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

## 4. Voice & Tone

**Voice in five words:** calm, literal, numerate, honest, respectful.

We sound like a competent lab tech who respects your intelligence — not a wellness influencer and not a luxury concierge. We state facts, show numbers, name limits, and stop. The confidence comes from the data being on the page, not from adjectives.

**Tone modulation by line:**
- **Foundations** — confident and established. These are well-studied molecules; we can use settled structure/function language ("supports normal energy metabolism," "supports muscle and nerve function").
- **Longevity** — the most conservative voice in the company. NAD+ / cellular-energy support only, always paired with the honesty clause. This is the highest enforcement-risk area and is scripted most cautiously.

### Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Magnesium Glycinate · 200 mg elemental" | "Premium high-absorption magnesium complex" |
| "Purity: ≥99% (assayed, see COA for Lot 2406A)" | "Lab-grade, ultra-pure, the purest available" |
| "Lot-matched Certificate of Analysis at hygieias.com/verify" | "Third-party tested" (no legal definition; weak claim) |
| "$0.58 / dose · $1.94 / gram — priced on formulation cost" | "Incredible value" / "luxury you deserve" |
| "Evidence Grade: Early. NMN is a NAD+ precursor; human trials are still small and functional outcomes are mostly not yet statistically significant." | "Turn back the clock" / "reverse aging" / "feel 10 years younger" |
| "Supports muscle and nerve function." (structure/function) | "Treats deficiency disease" / "prevents heart disease" (disease claims) |
| "We grade our own science — including the weak spots." | "Clinically proven to work" (unless RCT-grade substantiation is on file) |
| "Glycinate for absorption and GI tolerance — not cheap oxide." | "Doctor's secret formula" / "breakthrough proprietary blend" |
| "The research is real but still early — and we'll show you how early." | "Backed by science" (vague, unsubstantiated) |
| Numbers in monospace; forms named; limits stated | Hype adjectives, urgency, fear, vague superlatives, proprietary blends |

> *(The "$0.58 / dose · $1.94 / gram" above is the real one-time figure for the NMN 300 mg × 60 capsule, used as a live formatting example. Each product's own audited price/dose math is computed per SKU.)*

**Hard language rules (the compliance floor):**
- Structure/function only. No "diagnose, treat, cure, prevent."
- No anti-aging / longevity-as-lifespan / disease-prevention claims, ever.
- The DSHEA disclaimer accompanies every structure/function claim, per FDA labeling rules (21 CFR 101.93). **To confirm** the exact placement/linking implementation against current FDA guidance at launch. *(There is no verified "Dec 2025 FDA enforcement-discretion update" relaxing this in our research — the only Dec 2025 FDA action on file is the NMN NDI reinstatement; default to the conservative, disclaimer-with-every-claim posture.)*
- "Third-party tested" is banned as a hero claim — we name the actual certification or we say nothing.
- Every customer-facing asset (PDP, ad, email, influencer brief, Amazon A+) passes the claims-review gate before publishing.

## 5. Brand Architecture Summary

**Model: a BRANDED HOUSE.** One masterbrand — **HYGIEIAS** — over two named *collections* (lines, never separate brands):

- **HYGIEIAS Foundations** — daily essentials; the trust base and recurring-revenue / retention engine.
- **HYGIEIAS Longevity** — healthspan / NAD+ system; the halo and acquisition / AOV (Average Order Value) / margin engine. Home of the NMN flagship.

The deep-blue wordmark and the Hygieia (goddess of health) story live at the **masterbrand level and only there.** Line and product names stay ruthlessly literal — that literalness *is* the transparency (the anti-NIOD rule: never let an internal code be the consumer-facing name).

**The endorsement device across both lines is the shared transparency system itself** — the Five Decodes, /verify, and the Transparency Standard — not a separate corporate signature.

**Shared infrastructure (this is the actual moat — it concentrates in one name):**
- One COA / QA (Quality Assurance) program and the **/verify** lot-lookup portal — every SKU, both lines.
- One **Decoded Panel** template (the Five Decodes) — identical monospace block on every PDP and label.
- One published **Transparency Standard** governing all SKUs.
- One design system: navy ink `#1B2A4A`, orange accent `#D9531E`, monospace for all numbers/specs; one bottle/label kit (the label *text* is the only thing that changes — solving the no-photography constraint and scaling the line, on a planning target of ~15 SKUs, at near-zero design cost).
- One Stack / Regimen Builder spanning both lines.

**Line differentiation = ONE accent device, nothing more:** Foundations = navy-only label; Longevity = navy label + a single orange accent stripe. The stripe is the only visual tell, and it signals the two different claims/price postures.

**Naming system (two layers):**
- **Consumer name** = Ingredient + Form/Salt + Dose (e.g. *NMN 300 mg (≥99% beta)*, *Magnesium Glycinate 200 mg (elemental)*).
- **Operational SKU code** = [Line]-[Ingredient]-[Dose/Format] (e.g. `L-NMN-300` capsule, `L-NMN-P100` powder, `F-MAG-200`, `S-HEALTHSPAN`), with lot appended for the COA panel (`L-NMN-300 · Lot 2406A`). The code drives catalog/shelf logic and /verify lookup — never the primary name.

**Navigation = transparency-first, curated STACKS as the front door** (anti-paradox-of-choice): pre-built stacks + a guided Stack Builder up top; power-user axes underneath (by **Line**, by **Goal**, and the signature **by Proof** — filter/sort by "USP/NSF certified," "Evidence: Strong," "≥99% purity," "lowest price-per-dose"). Making transparency a *navigation axis* is what distinguishes HYGIEIAS from a generic transparency spine.

**Why branded house (decisive):** the entire moat *is* trust/transparency — a compounding asset that must concentrate in ONE name (PR, SEO, the COA program, the Transparency Standard, /verify, and the Evidence Grade all accrue to HYGIEIAS). A house-of-brands would force us to rebuild trust N times — capital-prohibitive for a startup and self-defeating when trust is the product. The two lines cross-subsidize trust: the Longevity halo (radical honesty where everyone overclaims) flows credibility *downward*; the everyday-verifiable Foundations anchor belief *upward*.

**The designed-for trade-off:** in a branded house, one bad batch hits all SKUs, and publishing a constitution raises the bar we're judged against. Therefore the per-lot COA, named certification, and full-label promise are not overhead — **they are the product.** QA is funded and staffed as the moat from day one. **NMN is shock-isolated** as a single ingredient line — its capsule and powder variants (e.g. `L-NMN-300` for the 300 mg × 60 capsule, plus the 100 g powder) — on a stable Foundations base, so a re-ban / Amazon delist / NDI problem damages the NMN line, not the rest of HYGIEIAS.

## 7. Trust Assets — COA / cGMP / NDI / Purity, and How We Use Them

Trust assets are not marketing decoration. In a branded house where the moat *is* trust, **the QA program is the product.** Each asset below is operationalized into the customer experience, not just held in a compliance file.

### COA (Certificate of Analysis) — the centerpiece
- **What:** a lot-matched lab report (purity + heavy metals + microbiology) for the exact batch the customer bought.
- **How used:** published via **/verify**, a house-wide lot-number lookup portal (the Nordic Naturals mechanic, but across the *whole* catalog — not just one category). Printed on every label: *"Verify this batch at hygieias.com/verify."* It is Decode #3 (THE PROOF) on every PDP.
- **Standard-bearing rule:** the **COA is posted before a batch ships.** This is the single most load-bearing operational commitment in the company and is written into the Transparency Standard.
- **Why it wins:** this is the exact asset premium incumbents consistently fail to provide consumer-facing (Thorne, Pure, Life Extension, NOW all gate, omit, or only-on-request — *internal assessment, to re-verify*). Because the FDA does not pre-approve supplements, a self-published lot-matched COA on the PDP is the strongest consumer-facing trust signal that exists.

### Purity %
- **What:** an assayed purity number, category-specific (e.g. ≥99% beta-NMN; omega-3 carries TOTOX + exact EPA/DHA mg; minerals disclose *elemental* mg).
- **How used:** Decode #2 (THE PURITY), shown as a number, never an adjective. Also a **browse-by-Proof navigation filter** ("≥99% purity"). Purity is the live competitive battleground for the NMN flagship.

### cGMP (current Good Manufacturing Practice)
- **What:** manufacturing in an FDA-registered cGMP facility — the table-stakes regulatory baseline and an Amazon marketplace requirement (third-party cGMP verification + lot-level documentation).
- **How used:** stated as baseline fact in the Transparency Standard and on the QA page; it underwrites the COA program. **To confirm:** the specific contract manufacturer and its cGMP registration / audit status before launch.

### NDI (New Dietary Ingredient) / NDIN (New Dietary Ingredient Notification)
- **What:** novel actives (NMN now; later urolithin A, spermidine) require a company-specific NDIN *or* sourcing from a supplier who filed one. **Self-affirmed GRAS does not suffice.** Mainstream vitamins/minerals/omega/creatine/magnesium are old/established — no NDI burden, which is why Foundations is Amazon-safe from day one.
- **How used (NMN, hard pre-launch gate):** secure a company-specific NDIN **or** contract a supplier already covered by an accepted NDIN. Following the FDA's NMN reversal (29 Sep 2025) and the Dec 2025 NDI reinstatements, two suppliers now have publicly posted FDA NDI letters — **SyncoZymes (NDI No. 1247)** and **Kingdomway (NDI No. 1259)**; sourcing from a supplier covered by one of these removes the need to self-file. Keep a documented pre-2022 "marketed as a supplement" evidence trail; reference the supplier-coverage statement on the PDP. **To confirm:** NDIN secured / supplier coverage on file — specifically whether our G1/G3 raw material is covered by #1247 or #1259. Launching NMN without this is a hard stop. (Regulatory context: FDA reversed its NMN exclusion on 29 Sep 2025 — lawful again, but a "mixed bag" with litigation expected and residual tail-risk.)

### Named third-party certification
- **What:** USP Verified / NSF (incl. Certified for Sport) / Clean Label Project — named, audited, ongoing certifications. Distinct from the empty phrase "third-party tested," which has no legal definition.
- **How used:** Decode #3, shown only **where actually held**, on a per-SKU basis, and as a browse-by-Proof filter. May help support a price premium that funds the QA moat (**To confirm** the actual premium magnitude — not yet sourced). **To confirm:** which certifications are pursued/held per SKU (e.g. USP/NSF on hero SKUs, Clean Label across the line).

### The three signature, hard-to-copy assets (the durable moat beyond the copyable COA mechanic)
1. **The HYGIEIAS Transparency Standard (v1.0)** — a short, public, versioned company constitution listing the self-imposed rules every SKU must meet (the Five Decodes + no proprietary blends + full label disclosure including inactives + COA published before a batch ships). Linked in the footer and on every PDP. **Stood up on day one — it is the launch, not a follow-on.** Turns transparency from a claim into an auditable promise.
2. **The Evidence Grade badge** — we publicly grade our own science and label weak items "Early," paired with the honesty clause: *"The research is real but still early — and we'll show you exactly how early."* Self-criticism is the one move hype competitors structurally cannot replicate, and the one differentiator that does not reduce to the copyable COA-on-page mechanic.
3. **/verify** — the house-wide lot-COA lookup portal, printed on every label, **stood up on day one.**

**The asset-to-bar trade-off (managed, not ignored):** publishing a constitution and grading our own science raises the standard we are judged against, and one missed COA or over-optimistic grade contradicts the published Standard across all SKUs. Mitigation is non-negotiable and operational: **never ship a batch before its COA posts; grade conservatively; fund and staff QA as the moat from day one.**

## 8. Brand Pillars

Five pillars carry the brand. Each is a belief, an operational commitment, and a customer-visible proof — not a slogan.

**Pillar 1 — Decoded by Default.**
*Belief:* the customer deserves the full spec, always. *Commitment:* the Five Decodes on every SKU, both lines, identical monospace panel. *Proof:* the Decoded Panel on every PDP and label; molecule + form + dose as the product name itself.

**Pillar 2 — Honest About the Evidence.**
*Belief:* overclaiming is the industry's original sin; we grade ourselves instead. *Commitment:* a public Evidence Grade (Strong / Moderate / Early) and the honesty clause on every Longevity PDP; we'd rather lose a sale than oversell. *Proof:* "Early"-graded items shown, not hidden; the one move hype brands cannot copy.

**Pillar 3 — Provable, Not Promised.**
*Belief:* trust is earned with documents, not adjectives. *Commitment:* lot-matched COA published before any batch ships; named certifications only; /verify on every label. *Proof:* the /verify portal and the COA-on-every-PDP — the asset incumbents gate or omit.

**Pillar 4 — Fair Price, Shown Math.**
*Belief:* price should reflect formulation cost, not marketing. *Commitment:* price-per-dose and price-per-gram computed and displayed; positioned between cheap-opaque and premium-gated; no luxury markup. *Proof:* Decode #4 on every PDP; the visible math is the anti-hype argument.

**Pillar 5 — One Name, One Standard.**
*Belief:* trust compounds when it concentrates. *Commitment:* a branded house under one masterbrand, one QA program, one published Transparency Standard governing every SKU across both lines. *Proof:* the versioned Transparency Standard in the footer and on every PDP; QA funded as the product, not overhead.
